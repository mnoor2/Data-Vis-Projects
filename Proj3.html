BMI vs Glucose

Analysis: Here, we compare the BMI vs the Glucose levels. We can see that for the most part, the more higher the glucose levels, the more older the participant is. Here, we can also see that participants with higher glucose levels tend to be diabetic however there are some inconsistent outliers that say otherwise. For the most part, we can see that testing glucose levels is a very trustable way to see if someone has diabetes or not.

BMI Distribution Across Age Groups

Analysis: Here, we can see the BMI distribution of each age group. We can see that the average BMI across all age groups tends to be near the 25-35 range. We can see that as the participants get older, they have more of a risk of a higher BMI which can lead to diabetes. Here, we can see that alot of the diabetic ranges in the BMI tend to be within this same range as well.

Average Glucose Across Age Groups

Analysis: From what we've seen, glucose tends to be the most reliable way to tell if someone has diabetes or not. Here, we can see that higher glucose levels usually means you are diabetic. The glucose levels tend to peak in the mid to early 60's with some noticable rises in the 30's. From what we've seen, we can confidently say that diabetes usually occurs in people with much higher glucose levels than normal.
